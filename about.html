About Me

Hello! I’m Anna-Maria Christodoulou, a PhD researcher, musician, and technologist. My work lies at the intersection of music and technology, where I explore how machine learning and multimodal information processing can deepen our understanding of music.

With a strong academic background in Music Technology and Artificial Intelligence, I aim to bridge the gap between scientific research and artistic expression. Whether it’s developing cutting-edge algorithms for music information retrieval (MIR) or working with creative music applications, I am deeply committed to fostering a more inclusive, accessible, and explainable music technology landscape.

Education and Experience

I hold a Master's degree in Music Technology from the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA), where I gained expertise in music programming, signal processing, and interactive systems. Currently, I’m pursuing my PhD at RITMO, University of Oslo, focusing on multimodal music understanding through deep learning.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I’ve worked as a Music Data Analyst at Masterchannelai and contributed as a Research Assistant to the MIRAGE project at RITMO, UiO. These roles have allowed me to combine my technical skills with my passion for music.

Beyond Research

As the founder of MUSAI Gr, I lead efforts to raise awareness about Music Information Retrieval (MIR) in Greece. This includes organizing events, tutorials, and discussions to make MIR approachable for both specialists and general audiences.

Outside academia, I’ve collaborated on creative projects such as documentary production for the Hellenic Parliament TV and live electronic music performances with the LEME Ensemble.
